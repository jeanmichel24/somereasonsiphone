Some reasons why you shouldn't buy a new iPhone 15 Pro Max

November, 21

By Jean Michel Shcherbak

The new iPhone 15 seems innovative compared to the iPhone 14: USB-C connector, "dynamic island" cutout in the middle of the screen, 48MP camera. A reason to upgrade your old iPhone? Here are a few reasons not to.

As of the time of writing, the author of this article cannot afford to buy a new iPhone yet. Credit Thenounproject.com

At the presentation on September 12, Apple presented a new generation of professional smartphones iPhone 15 Pro and iPhone 15 Pro Max. The update is difficult to call revolutionary, the devices differ from last year's models by only a small set of features. It is definitely not necessary to change from the iPhone 14 Pro for their sake, but you can switch to the new models from an older device.

Dynamic island

Apple has praised the camera of the iPhone 15 Pro so highly, yet it seems to have forgotten about the display. Yes, it has become brighter - 2000 nits under direct sunlight, so now you can easily browse memes while standing in the street in the summer. However, they did not add a 120Hz refresh rate, so we have to make do with the standard 60Hz.

USB-C connector

Yes, Apple has replaced the Lightning connector with USB-C. One of the key innovations in the lineup is the switch from Lightning to USB-C. This is a plus for those who work with other Apple products and third-party devices, such as Android smartphone s, on a daily basis. Additionally, you can now charge headphones and watches from your phone. High-speed data transfer should not be expected.

Camera

Both iPhone 14 and iPhone 15 can record 4K video at up to 60fps with HDR Dolby Vision, and they have similar capabilities in Cinematic mode, Action mode, and slo-mo video recording. The video recording capabilities are quite similar between the two models, which means if you're primarily interested in video, the iPhone 14 might still suffice

To take or not to take?
The main question - is it worth buying a new smartphone or is it better to stay this year without an upgrade? It seems that there are no serious reasons to move to the iPhone 15 Pro. Except in two cases. The first - you are a connoisseur of portable gaming, the second - you need optical zoom. If you have an iPhone 14 Pro or iPhone 14 Pro Max, there's no point in upgrading to the next generation - there aren't many changes. Even iPhone 13 Pro and 13 Pro Max owners can safely skip the 2023 lineup. The performance of the two-year-old models is still enough for everything.Other improvements will come to the iPhone 14 Pro along with the release of iOS 17.
